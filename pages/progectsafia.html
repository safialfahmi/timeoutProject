What To Do In NYC!!



A trip to New York City is the experience of a lifetime. With famous attractions like Times Square, Central Park, the Empire State Building and Yankee Stadium—to name just a few—NYC packs more to see and do into one compact area than any other place on earth. Each of the City’s five boroughs contains its own roster of must-see destinations


Event

.Some of the world’s most famous events—big festivals and holiday parades,
musical performances and art exhibitions

The Orchid Show



Each year, thousands of luscious blooms fill the Victorian-style Enid A. Haupt Conservatory at the New York Botanical Garden in the Bronx. A stroll through this gorgeous display is the perfect way to warm up on a brisk day; or on select evenings throughout the run of the show, enjoy tours and special performances. This year’s installment is a tribute to Singapore—one of the world’s most formidable orchid cultures—in partnership with Singapore Botanic Gardens and Gardens by the Bay.

Joan Miró: Birth of the World



Approximately 60 works of Spanish artist Joan Miró are presented in this MoMA exhibition, the centerpiece of which is his signature work, The Birth of the World. Drawn from the museum’s own collection with several loans, the exhibition includes paintings, prints, works on paper, illustrated books and objects that are all presented in relation to that landmark in order to shed light on the artist’s creative process.

Asia Week New York



For just over a week, Asian art and culture take over New York City. Asia Week is celebrating its 10th anniversary this time around, and among the museums , galleries, auction houses and cultural institutions participating are the Noguchi Museum, China Institute, The Met and the Rubin Museum of Art. Each venue will be showing works from the continent, and art dealers from around the world will display their collections during open houses throughout the week. There will also be a full schedule of films, lectures, symposia, curator talks , tours, auctions and other events


Sport

New York City is the world’s undisputed sports champion, with professional teams playing in every type of game you could want to watch.

New York Rangers Hockey



New York City where Bobby Thompson hit "audible shot" around the world "and where Willis Reid was camping on the pitch to play in the NBA Finals 7. With teams in every major sport, there are new classic athletic moments being created every day in the five neighborhoods. March 9 - April 5

New York Knicks Basketball



Manhattan’s NBA team boasts a rich history and promising young players like Frank Ntilikina, Kevin Knox and Dennis Smith Jr. The team’s rabid fan base always makes a game at Madison Square Garden feel like an event. Celebrities frequently sit courtside.

WrestleMania Week



Fans can not get enough interesting WWE stories and exciting sports, and RistleMania - the biggest league event of the year - brings with him a full week of activities at the Barclays Center as well as the main event at Metlive Stadium .


Nigth life

NYC is rightfully known for its nightlife.


Cielo



Opened by Nicolas Matar, resident DJ of the renowned club Pacha in Ibiza, Cielo hosts some of the best DJs on the planet and has a sound system that is every audiophile’s dream. The state-of-the-art club in the Meatpacking District features a sunken dance floor surrounded by banquettes and tables. The music is deeply soulful house along with various genres of electronic. Getting inside isn’t easy at this exclusive venue, but once in, you are a VIP, with no private rooms or checkpoints to pass.

The Bell House



This new bar and concert venue brings indie acts to the redecorated 1920s warehouse space, complete with chandeliers, lots of dark wood trimmings and an old-fashioned stage with red velvet curtains.

Break Bar & Billiards



There are lots of pool halls around, but not too many let you play for free (with the purchase of a drink). This killer happy -hour deal (also applicable to Ping-Pong) is one of the biggest draws at Break, a popular Astoria hangout. And its Queens location means Break is roomy compared with some of its Manhattan counterparts; in addition to pool and Ping-Pong, other forms of bar entertainment include Skee-Ball, foosball and arcade basketball.


Attraction

Breathtaking views from Top of the Rock, Statue of Liberty National Monument
and Times Square.

Times Square



Flashing neon lights and giant digital billboards. Brilliant Broadway marquees. Costumed characters and musicians. Times Square is big, bright and unforgettable. Its main junction is filled with popular retailers—plus the TKTS discount booth, which offers up to 50 percent off theater tickets. Walk to the top of its red steps—you may know them from the "Empire State of Mind" video—for a sweeping view of the area, including One Times Square, the building from which the ball drops on New Year's Eve. People-watchers will love the pedestrian-only zones furnished with tables and chairs.

Statue of Liberty National Monument



The Statue of Liberty is perhaps New York City's most familiar landmark and the easiest one to overlook since it's only accessible by boat. This historic monument has welcomed many generations of hopeful Americans to our shores. In order to visit the Statue of Liberty, you must purchase tickets through Statue Cruises—they are the only company licensed to sell tickets for the Statue.

Top of the Rock Observation Deck



Many people come to New York hoping to make it to the top, and the fastest way there is the elevator—to the 67th, 69th or 70th floors at 30 Rockefeller Plaza. The three 360-degree-view observation decks at Top of the Rock showcase the City’s spectacular skyline. Although you won’t be able to see the skating rink or Christmas tree from up high, be sure to visit these seasonal attractions when you come back down.
